---
title: Software “as a Tool” vs “as a Service”
date: 2026-02-26
summary: Pricing, usage and other considerations
---

| | **Software as a Tool (pay once)** | **Software as a Service (pay monthly)** |
|---|---|---|
| What it is | Something you buy and keep | Something you use continuously |
| Where it runs | On your device | On your device + online (provider runs it for you) |
| Cost logic | Mostly “build it once” | “Run it and maintain it every month” |
| Security & backups | Mostly on you | Included (the provider handles it) |
| Updates | Rare / optional | Regular, included |
| Support | Limited / optional | Ongoing, included |

---

## When monthly pricing makes sense
Monthly pricing is normal when there are ongoing costs:
- hosting/servers
- monitoring (to keep things working)
- security updates
- backups
- support
- improvements over time

---

## About “smart” (AI) features
Many subscription services include “smart” features (recommendations, assistants, automation, analytics) because they:
- require ongoing computing power
- improve over time
- depend on external systems that change

But in many cases, you do not actually need these things and they just clutter your life.
